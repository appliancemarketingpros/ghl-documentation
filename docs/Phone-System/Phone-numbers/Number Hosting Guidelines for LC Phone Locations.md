**Overview**

  


**Number Hosting**(Hosted SMS / Hosted Messaging) allows you to enable SMS/MMS messaging on phone numbers you already own with another carrier, while continuing to use LC for messaging.

  


Your phone number remains fully active with your original carrier for voice calls, but text messaging (SMS/MMS) is routed through LC.

  


This setup is ideal for businesses that want to keep their existing phone numbers while leveraging LC’s messaging capabilities.![](https://s3.amazonaws.com/cdn.freshdesk.com/data/helpdesk/attachments/production/155061748220/original/pGXKSlb9E2FOtPnMKfcLnTf81bqbWSWOHQ.png?1767340421)

## **What Is Number Hosting (Hosted SMS / Hosted Messaging)?**

  


Number Hosting enables SMS/MMS functionality on externally owned phone numbers without transferring voice services.

### Key Points

  * You **retain ownership** of your phone number with your current carrier

  * **Voice calling remains unchanged**

  * **Only SMS/MMS** is routed through LC (HighLevel) via Twilio

  * No porting of the number is required


### What This Means

  * Full control of your number stays with the original carrier

  * LC handles messaging delivery and compliance

  * Messaging reliability and throughput are managed by Twilio

## **Supported & Unsupported Number Types**

##   


## **Eligible Number Types**

  * **US and Canada Landline Numbers:** Most local landline and toll-free numbers in the US and Canada can be enabled for SMS hosting.

  * **Toll-Free Numbers:** Toll-free numbers from the US and Canada are generally supported.

  * **Numbers Without Existing SMS Service:** The number must not already have SMS enabled with another provider.


**Ineligible Number Types**

  * **Mobile Numbers:** Cellular numbers cannot be hosted for SMS through Twilio.

  * **Twilio Voice Numbers:** Numbers already owned by a Twilio account for voice services cannot be hosted for messaging on a different account.

  * **Non-US/Canada Numbers:** Hosted SMS is not supported for numbers outside the US and Canada.


  


## **How Number Hosting Works for LC Phone Locations**

  


To activate Hosted Messaging in LC, a **strict verification and authorization process** to prevent fraud and ensure carrier compliance is required.

### Required Steps (Completed by the End User / Number Owner)

  1. **Ownership Verification**

  2. **Letter of Authorization (LoA) Signing**

  3. **Carrier Confirmation** (if required by the provider)


Once Twilio approves these steps, **Hosted Messaging routing is activated** , allowing SMS/MMS to function inside the LC phone system.

## **What Happens Next?**

  


Once your **Location ID** and **phone number(s)** are submitted:

  1. HighLevel generates the **Hosted Messaging Verification Form**

  2. You or your client completes:

     * Ownership Verification

     * Letter of Authorization (LoA)

  3. Twilio reviews and approves the request

  4. SMS/MMS routing is activated for your LC phone system


* * *

## **Timeline Expectations**
