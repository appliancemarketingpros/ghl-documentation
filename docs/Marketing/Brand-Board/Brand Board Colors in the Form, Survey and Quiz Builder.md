# **What are Brand Board Colors in Form, Survey, and Quiz Builder?**

  


Brand Board colors in the Form, Survey, and Quiz Builder let you use saved brand colors directly from the color picker while styling builder elements. This helps you keep buttons, inputs, labels, and other design elements aligned with your brand without copying and pasting color codes manually. 

  


Brand Colors appear based on your saved Brand Board setup. If you do not see them, confirm that your Brand Board has colors configured and that the correct default Brand Board is set. 

  


[Click here to learn more about creating brand boards](<https://help.gohighlevel.com/en/support/solutions/articles/155000003136>).

## **Key Benefits of Brand Board Colors in Form, Survey, and Quiz Builder**

  


  * **Faster Styling:** Apply saved brand colors without manually entering hex codes each time.  
  


  * **Better Brand Consistency:** Keep buttons, fields, labels, and other visual elements aligned with your approved color palette.  
  


  * **Simpler Collaboration:** Give teams a shared set of reusable colors across Forms, Surveys, and Quizzes.  
  


  * **Easier Color Management:** Open Brand Settings from the picker to add or manage reusable custom colors.


* * *

## **How Brand Board Colors Work in the Builder**

  


This feature connects your saved brand palette to the Builder so you can apply approved colors without interrupting your workflow. It also reduces the need to manually match colors across forms and other assets.

  


When you open a color field in the Builder:

  


  1. The color picker displays available color sections, including Brand Colors, Global Custom Colors, and Default Colors.  
  


  2. Selecting a saved Brand Color applies that color to the chosen element. The Builder shows the correct color preview automatically after selection.  
  


  3. To add a new custom brand color, click Manage from the color picker to open Brand Settings.


  

    
    
    **Note:** Changing your default Brand Board does not automatically restyle existing Forms, Surveys, or Quizzes. Existing assets keep their current styling until you update them manually.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

  


**Q: Why are my Brand Colors not showing in the color picker?**  
Confirm that your Brand Board has saved colors and that the correct default Brand Board is configured.

  


**Q: What is the difference between Brand Colors and Global Custom Colors?**  
Brand Colors come from your Brand Board’s approved palette. Global Custom Colors are reusable saved colors that can be managed from Brand Settings and used across supported color pickers.

  


**Q: Will changing my default Brand Board automatically update my existing forms?**  
No. Existing Forms, Surveys, and Quizzes keep their current styling until you update them manually.
