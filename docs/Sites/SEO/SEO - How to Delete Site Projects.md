# **What is Deleting****SEO****Site Projects?**

  


Deleting a Site Project permanently removes its audits and automatically reverses SEO fixes applied through that project, while immediately restoring your available quota so you can add a new site.

  

    
    
    **Note:** Deleting a Site Project is **permanent** and **cannot** **be** **undone**.
    

* * *

## **Key Benefits of Deleting a Site Project**

  


  * **Quota relief:** Instantly frees a slot if you’ve reached the four-site cap.  
  

  * **Operational agility:** Quickly rotate in new client sites or higher-priority properties.  
  

  * **Clean rollback:** Auto-applied SEO fixes from the project are reverted to avoid lingering changes.  
  

  * **Clarity:** Keeps your SEO workspace focused on active, relevant domains.


* * *

## **How To Delete a SEO Site Project**

  


Following these steps ensures a clean, predictable removal with immediate quota restoration and automatic rollback of applied fixes.

  


  1. Go to **Sites** > **SEO**.  
  
![](https://jumpshare.com/share/v5ZeGdULNUEzEWWayMRx+/Screen+Shot+2026-01-28+at+7.32.45+PM.png)  
  

  2. Open the **Site** **Projects** **list** and locate the project to remove.  
  

  3. Click the **three dots** (⋯) next to the project name and click on **Delete Project**.  
  
![](https://jumpshare.com/share/3gI7uM0eZc47hYsJg40N+/Screen+Shot+2026-01-28+at+7.35.23+PM.png)  
  

  4. **Review** the **confirmation** **modal** and read the **impact** **summary** (quota restored, fixes reverted, action is permanent).  
  

  5. Click on the **Delete** **Project** button.  
  
![](https://jumpshare.com/share/vRyz7Te7RVzLMHPhtumg+/Screen+Shot+2026-01-28+at+7.40.46+PM.png)

## **SEO Fixes Rollback**

  


Understanding what is reverted prevents unexpected changes on live assets and clarifies what you may need to re-apply manually.

  


Deleting a Site Project reverts SEO fixes applied through that project, including:

  


  * Funnel path fixes
  * Blog path fixes
  * External fixes (automated adjustments triggered by the project)
  * Manually made edits that are unrelated to the project’s auto-applied fixes typically remain.


  


After deletion, consider re-validating key items (redirects, sitemaps, Search Console coverage) if you still need them.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

  


**Q: Can Support restore a deleted Site Project?**

No. Deletion is permanent and cannot be reversed.

  


**Q: Will meta titles/descriptions I changed manually revert?**

Only fixes applied through the Site Project are reverted. Manual edits unrelated to those fixes typically remain.

  


**Q: Who can delete a Site Project?**

Users with sufficient permissions (typically Admins or those with Sites/SEO access) can delete.

  


**Q: Can I re-add the same domain after deletion?**

Yes. Once quota is restored, you can add the domain as a new Site Project and start a fresh audit.

  


**Q: Does deletion affect live funnels/blogs beyond the project’s fixes?**

It removes the project’s auto-applied fixes. Other unrelated content and manual edits remain unchanged.

  


**Q: How can I verify quota settings?**

You can verify quota under **SEO** > **Settings** > **Quota Usage**.
