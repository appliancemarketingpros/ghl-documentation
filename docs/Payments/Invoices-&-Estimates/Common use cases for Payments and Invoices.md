**Source URL:** [https://help.gohighlevel.com/support/solutions/articles/48001220600-common-use-cases-for-payments-and-invoices](https://help.gohighlevel.com/support/solutions/articles/48001220600-common-use-cases-for-payments-and-invoices)  
**Category:** Payments  
**Folder:** Invoices & Estimates

---

Payments are used to pay for goods or services. They can be made by credit card, debit card, bank transfer, cheque, cash, or any other method of payment. Invoices are used to record the amount that has been paid for a product or service. The invoice is usually sent with the payment so that it can be tracked as part of the accounting process.

  


In this article we will go over some common or popular use cases for using payments and invoices.

## **Using invoices for recording manual payments _without_ Stripe Connect**

**Please Note:**

If you opt to use invoicing without Stripe Connect you will are required to manually record and collect payments.  
For now, recording manual payments without Stripe Connect works only for one-time invoices.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

  


**Q:****Can I use GoHighLevel payments to track partial payments for invoices?**

Yes, HighLevel allows you to track partial payments. You can create an invoice with the full amount and update it each time a partial payment is made. This helps you keep accurate records of outstanding balances.

  


  


**Q:**Is it possible to customize the appearance of invoices sent to clients?****

Currently, HighLevel provides a standard invoice template. While you can add custom details like your business name, logo, and notes, more advanced customization options, such as adjusting layouts or colors, are not available at this time.

  


  


**Q:**Can I set up recurring invoices for subscription-based services?****

The text here has no limits or restrictions. You should add as much text as needed to explain the answer to this Yes, you can create recurring invoices for subscription-based services. While HighLevel doesn’t have an automated recurring invoice feature, you can duplicate a previously sent invoice and set reminders to send it periodically.

  


  


**Q:**How can I integrate third-party payment processors?****

HighLevel supports integration with popular payment processors like Stripe and PayPal. You’ll need to connect your account via the integrations section in the platform. Once linked, you can accept payments directly through your invoices.

  


Q: Why does my payment page say "Live Mode" even after I clicked "Test"? 

This usually means your actual Stripe account is still in Live Mode. Log into your Stripe Dashboard and toggle the "Test Mode" switch in the top right corner. Then, refresh your HighLevel page.

  


**Q: Why does an invoice show “Payment Processing” even after a client has paid?**

If your invoice shows “Payment Processing,” it means the payment has been initiated but not yet settled. This typically happens with ACH bank transfers or payment methods like PayPal eCheck, which require 2–5 business days to clear. Once the payment is successfully processed, the invoice status will automatically update to “Paid.”

  


Q: I sent an invoice, but my client says the link is broken or they can't click it to pay. What happened and how do I fix it?

The payment link is disabled because the invoice is past its scheduled due date. The system automatically deactivates the payment option when the payment date has already passed.

To fix it: 

  1. you must send a new invoice with a corrected schedule:
  2. Duplicate (Clone) the original invoice.
  3. Adjust the Payment Schedule dates to a current or future date.
  4. Resend the new invoice to your client. The link will then be active.


  


Q: Why can’t I void or delete invoices that were marked as paid (even if they were just test/demo payments)?  
In GoHighLevel, an invoice cannot be voided if it is already marked as paid, even if the payment was created for testing or demo purposes. The system requires you to refund the payment first before the invoice can be voided.  
How to fix it:

  1. Go to the invoice and click the three dots (⋮).

  2. Select View Transaction.

  3. Click Refund and complete the refund.

  4. Go back to the invoice.

  5. Click the three dots (⋮) again and select Void.Once refunded, the invoice can be successfully voided and will no longer reflect as an active or paid invoice.  
Notes:


  * Paid invoices cannot be deleted directly.

  * Demo or test payments that are not clearly marked as Test Mode behave like real payments in the system.

  * Refunding ensures the invoice does not affect records, reports, or client visibility.

  * Only agency-level users (Agency Owner/Admin/User) can contact GoHighLevel support—sub-account users cannot.


  


### Q: Why does the payment date disappear and show an “Invalid Date” error when recording a manual payment?  
This issue occurs when the manual payment date entered is outside the system’s accepted date range or conflicts with the invoice timeline, causing the platform to reject it and clear the field.

Why it happens:

  * The invoice was issued on a later date, but the payment date is being set far in the past.

  * The system validates payment dates against the invoice’s issue and status dates.

  * Language/locale settings can sometimes trigger validation issues, but this is not the primary cause.


How to fix it:

  * Ensure the payment date is on or after the invoice issue date.

  * Refresh the page before recording the payment.

  * Try recording the payment with a date that aligns with the invoice timeline.

  * If needed, temporarily switch the account language to English and retry.


Q: Why can’t I make a partial payment on an invoice?  
You may not be able to make a partial payment if the invoice already has a payment schedule (installments) set. The system does not allow partial payments in this case because it would conflict with the scheduled payments.  
To Fix

  * Remove or modify the payment schedule. 
  * Then go to the invoice and select Record Payment → Record manually to enter a partial payment.


  


Q: Why does my processing fee show on one invoice but not another?  
Processing fees configured in Settings → Charges are not automatically applied to existing invoices. To include the new fee option, you must recreate any invoices made before the setting was enabled. The fee option will only be visible and applicable to invoices created after the change is saved in settings.
